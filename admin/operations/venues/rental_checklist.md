**CAN.dy Dance Project — Venue/Studio Rental Checklist**  
Use for studios, theaters, bars/clubs, event organizers, and filming locations.

### A. Basics to confirm (before you commit)
- **Date(s)/times:** load-in, rehearsal, show, strike; overtime rates  
- **Address + access:** parking, entry instructions, ADA access  
- **Space specs:** dimensions, floor type, mirrors, sound system, lighting  
- **Capacity/occupancy limit** and fire code rules  
- **What’s included:** chairs, tables, dressing room, storage, water, security

### B. Money terms
- **Total fee** and what it covers  
- **Deposit amount** + due date  
- **Payment method** accepted  
- **Refund/cancellation policy** (and deadlines)  
- **Reschedule policy** (illness, emergencies, venue issues)  
- **Damages/cleaning fees** and what counts as damage

### C. Insurance & liability (often the biggest “gotcha”)
- Do they require **general liability insurance**? Amount? (e.g., $1M)  
- Do they require you to name them as **additional insured**?  
- Are you agreeing to **indemnify/hold harmless** them? (Try to keep mutual and reasonable.)  
- Are there waivers they require participants to sign?

### D. Rules & restrictions
- **Shoes/heels rules**, rosin/chalk, floor protection, chair use  
- **Noise limits** / curfew  
- **Alcohol policy** (if venue)  
- **Filming/photography permissions** (important for video projects)  
- **Closed rehearsal/privacy** expectations  
- **Minors policy** (you’re 18+, but venues may still ask)

### E. Technical / production (for performances)
- **Sound:** who runs music? input options (aux/Bluetooth/XLR), soundcheck time  
- **Lighting:** basic wash vs cues; who operates; extra fees  
- **Backstage:** dressing rooms, green room, storage, call time  
- **Front-of-house:** ticketing, door staff, ushers, merchandise table  
- **Security:** who provides it and when required

### F. Key clauses to watch in the contract (ask to edit if needed)
- **Who is the contracting party:** “CAN.dy Dance Project (unincorporated association)” and not an individual, if possible  
- **Personal guarantee:** avoid clauses that make an individual personally liable  
- **Automatic renewals**  
- **One-sided cancellation** (venue can cancel anytime but you can’t)  
- **Broad indemnity** (you cover everything even if it’s their fault)  
- **Use of your likeness/content:** ensure you keep rights to your footage  
- **Force majeure** language (fires, outages, etc.)—make sure deposits aren’t automatically forfeited

### G. Internal CAN.dy steps (recommended)
- Get written approval from **both Co-Directors** before signing/committing money (per bylaws).  
- Store signed agreements in the shared drive; Secretary keeps the record.  
- Add key dates to the project calendar (payment deadlines, cancellation windows).

## Tailored add-ons for theaters + studios + bars (plus email templates)

### 1) Quick “red flags” by venue type

**Dance studios**
- Cancellation terms that forfeit the entire package if you reschedule.
- Extra charges for **heels/chairs** or restrictions on chair use.
- “No filming” clauses (conflicts with your recording-as-condition rule).
- Studio requiring the account be under an individual’s name (try to contract as “CAN.dy Dance Project, an unincorporated association”).

**Theaters**
- Hidden labor costs: house manager, technician minimums, load-in/out overtime.
- Ticketing/box office fees, merch splits, alcohol/bar revenue terms.
- Insurance requirements (often $1M–$2M) + additional insured.
- Strict marketing/content approvals; restrictions on recording the performance.

**Bars/clubs**
- Age/ID policy for performers + rehearsal call times (21+ venue even if performers are 18+).
- Revenue split language (door vs bar minimum guarantees).
- Sound levels/curfews; security staffing requirements.
- Strong indemnity/hold harmless clauses; sometimes “you’re responsible for patron behavior” language.

---

## 2) Standard negotiation asks (safe to request)
- Add: “CAN.dy Dance Project (unincorporated association)” as renter/producer.
- Add: **mutual** cancellation/force majeure; allow reschedule with credit.
- Limit your indemnity to “to the extent caused by your group” and exclude their negligence.
- Confirm in writing: **filming/photography permitted** (or specify exactly what is allowed).
- Confirm: chair use, heels allowed, floor protection requirements.
