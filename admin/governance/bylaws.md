## CAN.dy Dance Project Bylaws

**CAN.dy Dance Project Bylaws**  
Adopted: _________, 2026  
Location: San Jose, Santa Clara County, California

### Article 1 — Name
The name of this unincorporated association is **CAN.dy Dance Project** (“CAN.dy” or the “Team”).

### Article 2 — Purpose
CAN.dy is a project-based community dance team that showcases sensual movement, including but not limited to heels and chair dance, through rehearsals and project-based live performances and video productions.

### Article 3 — Nonprofit intent (informal)
CAN.dy is organized for community/artistic purposes and is not intended to distribute profits to individuals. Reasonable payments and reimbursements for Team expenses are permitted as provided in these bylaws.

### Article 4 — Participation eligibility
1. **Age.** Participation is limited to individuals **18 years or older**.  
2. **Project casting.** Auditions and casting for any Project are at the discretion of the **Project Choreographer(s)**.  
3. **General participation.** There is no audition requirement to be a general Team participant, subject to conduct and safety rules and project needs.

### Article 5 — Leadership structure (Officers)
1. **Co-Directors.** The Team will have **two (2) Co-Directors**. Co-Directors are **not elected positions**.  
2. **Treasurer.** The Team will have a **Treasurer**.  
3. **Secretary.** The Team will have a **Secretary**.  
4. **Leadership Team.** The Co-Directors, Treasurer, and Secretary constitute the **Leadership Team**.

### Article 6 — Officer duties
1. **Co-Directors (joint).** The Co-Directors are responsible for overall direction and management of CAN.dy, including: selecting Projects; approving fundraisers and gigs; managing external relationships and contracts; enforcing safety/brand standards; and supervising appointed roles.  
2. **Treasurer.** The Treasurer is responsible for financial administration, including: maintaining the Team’s bank account(s); processing payments; tracking income/expenses by Project; handling reimbursements; ensuring required approvals are obtained; and providing monthly financial reports to the Leadership Team.  
3. **Secretary.** The Secretary is responsible for administrative records, including: maintaining the bylaws and key policies; keeping a roster of officers and members in good standing; keeping minutes or written consents for Leadership actions; and maintaining copies of key agreements.  
4. **Delegation.** Officers may delegate tasks but remain responsible for oversight within their scope.

### Article 7 — Officers: eligibility, elections, terms, and vacancies
1. **Member in good standing (for voting/office).** A “member in good standing” is an individual who:  
   a) has participated in **at least two (2) Projects in the last twelve (12) months**, and  
   b) is not currently suspended/removed and has satisfied any current financial obligations to the Team.  
2. **Eligible voters.** Only members in good standing may vote in officer elections and tie-break votes.  
3. **Eligibility to run for office (Treasurer/Secretary).** Any member in good standing may run for Treasurer or Secretary, **plus** any additional candidate approved unanimously by both Co-Directors.  
4. **Elections (Treasurer and Secretary).** The **Treasurer** and **Secretary** will be elected by members in good standing pursuant to the CAN.dy Officer Election Procedure, and elections will be held **annually in November**.  
5. **Co-Directors not elected.** **Co-Directors are not elected positions.** Co-Director selection, continuation, resignation, and replacement are governed by Section 7.6.  
6. **Co-Director selection and replacement.** The individuals serving as Co-Directors at the time these bylaws are adopted will continue as Co-Directors unless they resign or are replaced. A Co-Director may be replaced only by **unanimous written agreement of both Co-Directors** (or, if a Co-Director position is vacant, by the written appointment of the remaining Co-Director). The Co-Directors may also unanimously appoint an additional Co-Director if desired, effective upon written notice.  
7. **Terms.** The Treasurer and Secretary serve until the next November election or until resignation/removal.  
8. **Vacancies (Treasurer/Secretary).** If the Treasurer or Secretary role becomes vacant between elections, the Co-Directors may appoint an interim officer until the next November election (or until a special election is held, if the Co-Directors call one).

### Article 8 — Projects; Project Choreographers (“Artistic Directors”)
1. **Terminology.** “Project Choreographer” and “Artistic Director” are interchangeable terms in CAN.dy.  
2. **Project proposals.** CAN.dy will accept project proposals on an ongoing basis from any person(s).  
3. **Project selection timing.** The Co-Directors will select the next Project from the proposal pool **no later than one (1) month before the end of the current Project**, when practicable.  
4. **Appointment.** Project Choreographer(s) are appointed by mutual agreement of the Co-Directors on a per-project basis.  
5. **Creative authority.** Subject to Co-Director veto rights in Section 8.6, the Project Choreographer(s) have creative and operational authority for their Project, including: casting; rehearsal count and frequency; attendance policy for the Project (including stricter project-specific requirements if desired); and setting the Project date.  
6. **Co-Director veto (safety/budget/brand).** The Co-Directors retain veto authority over Project decisions necessary to address:  
   a) participant **safety**, including physical safety and harassment prevention;  
   b) **budget/financial** constraints; and  
   c) Team **brand/reputation** and public-facing risks.

### Article 9 — Decision-making and tie-break voting
1. **Regular Leadership Team meetings.** The Leadership Team will meet at least **monthly**. The regular target meeting date is the **last Sunday of each month**, unless the Leadership Team decides otherwise. Regular Leadership Team meetings are open to any interested members, except that the Leadership Team may close any portion of a meeting for sensitive matters.  
2. **Leadership decisions.** Ordinary Team business may be decided by majority vote of the Leadership Team, except where these bylaws require Co-Director approval.  
3. **Co-Director disagreement.** If the Co-Directors disagree on any decision (business or creative), the decision will be submitted to a vote of eligible voters under Section 9.4, unless a decision is required immediately for safety.  
4. **Eligible voters (tie-break).** Eligible voters are **members in good standing** as defined in Article 7.  
5. **Voting method and threshold.** Votes may be conducted online. The decision passes by a **simple majority of votes cast**. Voting will remain open for **48 hours** from notice.  
6. **Notice.** The Leadership Team will provide the question to be voted on and relevant context in writing at the start of the 48-hour window.  
7. **Emergency safety exception.** For immediate safety issues, either Co-Director may pause a rehearsal/activity pending rapid Leadership review; the tie-break vote may follow if needed.

### Article 10 — Dues, payments, refunds
1. **Project-based dues formula.** Dues are set per Project as follows:
   - **Dancers:** (Number of scheduled rehearsals) × **$20**
   - **Project Choreographer(s)/Artistic Director(s):** (Number of scheduled rehearsals) × **$10**
2. **Due date.** Dues are due **one (1) week before** the first rehearsal of the Project, unless the Co-Directors state otherwise in writing for that Project.  
3. **Nonpayment.** If dues are not paid within **one (1) week after** the due date, the participant’s spot may be forfeited and removal from the Project is automatic.  
4. **Refunds.** If a participant drops out, CAN.dy may provide **prorated refunds** based on rehearsals attended before withdrawal, as determined by the Treasurer consistent with available funds and any nonrefundable expenses already incurred.  
5. **Future adjustments.** The Leadership Team may adopt sliding scale or hardship accommodations in the future if funds allow.

### Article 11 — Finances and controls
1. **Approving fundraisers and gigs.** Any fundraiser or paid gig/event commitment requires approval of **both Co-Directors**.  
2. **Authorized signers.** The bank account will have the **two Co-Directors and the Treasurer** as authorized signers (as the bank allows).  
3. **Studio fees.** The Treasurer may pay **studio fees** without additional approval.  
4. **All other spending.** Any non-studio-fee spending requires **authorization from both Co-Directors** in writing (message/email is acceptable).  
5. **Reimbursements.** Reimbursements are allowed with a receipt and/or proof of purchase submitted within **30 days** of the expense.  
6. **Monthly reporting.** The Treasurer will provide a **monthly** report to the Leadership Team showing balances and recent transactions.  
7. **Officer reimbursement approval (conflict control).** Reimbursements to any officer require approval by **both Co-Directors**. Reimbursements to a Co-Director require approval by the **other Co-Director** and the Treasurer. No person may approve their own reimbursement.  
8. **Project budgets (recommended).** For each Project, the Co-Directors (with input from the Project Choreographer(s) and Treasurer) may set a Project budget. CAN.dy will not intentionally approve Project expenses that exceed expected Project income unless both Co-Directors approve a written exception.  
9. **Access to financial records.** The Treasurer will provide reasonable read-only access to financial reports/records to the Co-Directors and Secretary for oversight and continuity.

### Article 12 — Payment platforms and reimbursements
1. **Dues collection.** Dues should be collected via **WildApricot** (or a successor platform approved by the Co-Directors) when feasible.  
2. **No peer-to-peer payments.** CAN.dy will avoid peer-to-peer payment services for Team transactions when feasible.  
3. **If an individual pays on behalf of the Team.** If a participant or officer must pay a Team expense personally (e.g., to secure time-sensitive studio costs), they may request reimbursement under Article 11.5, subject to required approvals and available funds.

### Article 13 — Appointed roles (including Social Media Manager, Outreach Lead, and Project Coordinator)
1. **Appointment.** The Co-Directors may appoint non-officer roles on a **per-Project or ongoing basis** and define the scope in writing where practicable.  
2. **Project Coordinator.** The Co-Directors may appoint a **Project Coordinator** to support the Project Choreographer(s) and help execute Projects. Unless otherwise defined in writing for a specific Project, the Project Coordinator’s responsibilities may include:  
   a) coordinating schedules, rehearsal logistics, and project calendars;  
   b) serving as a primary liaison with venues, studios, and event organizers;  
   c) coordinating day-of logistics and communications; and  
   d) supporting administrative needs of the Project Choreographer(s) and Co-Directors.  
   The Project Coordinator does **not** have independent authority to bind CAN.dy to contracts or spending commitments unless expressly authorized in writing by both Co-Directors.  
3. **Social Media Manager — approval during probation.** For the first **one (1) month** after appointment, the Social Media Manager must obtain approval from at least **one (1) Co-Director** before posting content on Team channels.  
4. **After probation.** After the probationary period, the Social Media Manager may post without prior approval while still adhering to Team standards and any Co-Director direction.  
5. **Outreach Lead.** The Co-Directors may appoint an **Outreach Lead** to support community outreach, partnership cultivation, and external relationship follow-up. Unless otherwise defined in writing, the Outreach Lead’s responsibilities may include:  
   a) responding to general outreach inquiries and partnership leads;  
   b) coordinating sponsor, collaborator, or community follow-up;  
   c) supporting event/community promotion in coordination with the Co-Directors and Social Media Manager; and  
   d) helping maintain outreach records, contact lists, or opportunity trackers.  
   The Outreach Lead does **not** have independent authority to bind CAN.dy to contracts, publicity commitments, or spending commitments unless expressly authorized in writing by both Co-Directors.  
6. **Removal.** Appointed roles may be ended by either Co-Director, or by majority vote of the Leadership Team.

### Article 14 — Conduct, consent, and rehearsal policies
1. **Respect & communication.** Participants must treat others with respect and communicate professionally.  
2. **Consent-based partnering.** All partnering/contact choreography must be **consent-based**; anyone may decline any contact without retaliation.  
3. **Base attendance notice.** Dancers must provide at least **two (2) hours’ notice** if they will miss a rehearsal, unless notice is not reasonably possible due to illness, injury, or other serious unforeseen circumstance. Project Choreographer(s) may adopt a stricter attendance policy for a specific Project.  
4. **Closed rehearsals.** Rehearsals are closed to nonparticipants unless the Project Choreographer(s) and Co-Directors approve otherwise.  
5. **Further code of conduct.** The Leadership Team may adopt a more detailed code of conduct consistent with these bylaws.

### Article 15 — Discipline, dismissal, and appeal
1. **Behavioral dismissal.** Dismissing someone from the Team or a Project for behavioral issues requires a **unanimous** decision of the Project Choreographer(s) and both Co-Directors.  
2. **Nonpayment dismissal.** Dismissal for nonpayment is handled under Article 10.  
3. **One appeal.** A dismissed participant may submit **one appeal**. The appeal may be made **in writing or in person**. After review, the same decision-makers will **revote** once; that outcome is final.

### Article 16 — Media release (photo/video)
1. **Consent to recording.** CAN.dy projects may involve photo/video recording during rehearsals, performances, and shoots.  
2. **Grant of rights.** By participating, individuals grant CAN.dy permission to use their name, image, likeness, and performance in photo/video captured in connection with CAN.dy activities for Team promotion, documentation, and distribution (including social media), without additional compensation.  
3. **Condition of participation.** Participation in CAN.dy activities/projects is conditioned on consenting to photo/video recording and the media release in this Article; individuals who do not consent may not participate.

### Article 17 — Dissolution
1. **Approval to dissolve.** CAN.dy may be dissolved upon written approval of both Co-Directors and confirmation by Leadership Team vote.  
2. **Winding up.** Upon dissolution, the Leadership Team will:  
   a) pay or provide for outstanding debts and obligations; then  
   b) donate any remaining funds to a **charity selected by the Leadership Team prior to dissolution**.  
3. **No private distribution.** Remaining funds will not be distributed to individuals, except for legitimate final reimbursements or refunds already owed under these bylaws.

### Article 18 — Amendments
These bylaws may be amended only with the approval of **both Co-Directors**. If the Co-Directors disagree, the tie-break vote process in Article 9 applies.

### Article 19 — Contract and commitment authority
1. **Who may sign.** Only a Co-Director may sign contracts or make binding commitments on behalf of CAN.dy, unless both Co-Directors provide written authorization naming another person and defining the scope.  
2. **Required review.** Before signing venue/studio/event agreements (or any agreement involving money, cancellation fees, insurance requirements, or publicity rights), the signing Co-Director will share the agreement with the other Co-Director for review when practicable.  
3. **Limits.** The Project Coordinator and other appointed roles may communicate with venues and organizers but may not bind CAN.dy without written authorization under Section 1.
